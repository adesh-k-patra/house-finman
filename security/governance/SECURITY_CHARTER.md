# Enterprise Security Charter

**Effective Date:** 2024-10-27
**Owner:** Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
**Approver:** Board of Directors / CEO
**Classification:** Internal

---

## 1. Mandate & Purpose
The Board and Executive Management of **HouseFin Man** hereby establish the Information Security Program. This program is mandated to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of the organization's data, systems, and reputation.

Information Security is a business enabler, not merely a technical function. Its purpose is to ensure trust in our platform, compliance with regulatory obligations, and resilience against evolving cyber threats.

## 2. Scope
This charter applies to all employees, contractors, vendors, and third parties accessing HouseFin Man systems or data. It covers all information assets, including customer data, intellectual property, financial records, and operational infrastructure.

## 3. Authority & Governance Structure

### 3.1 Board of Directors
- **Responsibility:** Ultimate oversight of cyber risk.
- **Action:** Reviews high-level risk reports quarterly; approves the Annual Risk Appetite Statement.

### 3.2 Chief Executive Officer (CEO)
- **Responsibility:** Executive accountability for security.
- **Action:** Empowers the CISO; ensures security is integrated into business strategy.

### 3.3 Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
- **Authority:** The CISO has the independent authority to:
    - Halt any project or deployment that presents an unacceptable risk to the organization.
    - Conduct investigations into security breaches and policy violations.
    - define and enforce security policies and standards.
- **Reporting:** Direct reporting line to the CEO (or CIO with dotted line to Risk Committee).

### 3.4 Security Steering Committee (SSC)
- **Members:** CISO, CTO, COO, Legal Counsel, HR Director.
- **Purpose:** Monthly forum to review risks, approve major security initiatives, and resolve conflicts between business speed and security controls.

## 4. Core Principles
1.  **Security by Design:** Security requirements must be defined *before* code is written or vendors are procured.
2.  **Least Privilege:** Access is granted only as needed for a specific role and time.
3.  **Zero Trust:** Verification is required for every request, regardless of network location.
4.  **Risk-Based Approach:** Resources are prioritized based on the potential impact and likelihood of threats.

## 5. Compliance & Enforcement
Adherence to security policies is a condition of employment. Violations will result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination and legal prosecution.
